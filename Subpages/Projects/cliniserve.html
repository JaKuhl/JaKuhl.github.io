    Redesign: Patient App

    Redesign: Patient App

    With Cliniserve's Patient App, patients receiving hospital care can send requests to nursing staff. After several years of active use on the units within different hospitals, the overall look and structure of the app should be adapted and improved.

    Client

    Role

    Intern (Product Management)

    Services

    Wireframing

    Prototyping

    User testing

    The story behind the project

    During my practical semester in the product management department of Cliniserve, I had the opportunity to get to grips with the Patient App. It is used in various German hospitals.

    There are two different versions of the Patient App: on the one hand, there is the mobile smartphone app, and on the other, the so-called terminal app. This terminal app is used by patients who have a so-called bedside terminal. These terminals are tablets that are fixed to the corresponding hospital bed. Cliniserve has received an increasing amount of negative feedback, particularly with regard to the terminal app, which is why the product team decided to initiate a comprehensive redesign of the app.

    I was allowed to use my time as an intern and help the product team with this redesign. I would like to explain below how the entire project went, what the goals were and what challenges we encountered.

    Project goal

    Based on feedback from clients and caregivers, Cliniserve was able to identify several "pain points".


    Painpoints:

    1. Queries difficult to read
    2. Use of keyboard and scroll animations very error-prone
    3. Switching between already made requests and the menu not intuitive
    4. Uncertainty of patients whether a request has already been sent

    The aim of the project was to create a new design for the terminal app, taking these pain points into account, to test it and to prepare it for implementation.

    Process

    After a short introduction to the objectives of the project, I received sketches from my teacher, which helped me to get a good understanding of the initial thoughts and ideas for the redesign. I also took some time to get a clear picture of the first ideas and to write down some ideas myself.

    After the first ideas were evaluated and we agreed on concrete ideas as a product team, I began to build the first wireframes in Adobe Xd. At the beginning, the focus was exclusively on the placement of the various components. The aesthetic aspects were to be added later. After the first wireframes were finished and we could get a good feeling for the handling of the new ideas, we as a team noticed improvements again and again. I corrected these ASAP. After several rounds of improvements, the new structure of the app was finalised. Due to time pressure, my guidance then took over the aesthetic design of the various screens, so that we could present finished screens for the construction of a prototype.

    I was allowed to build a prototype with the finished wireframes, which was to be used for user testing. For this "user testing" I developed three different scenarios and created a user flow accordingly. Based on the already completed screens, I built individual screens together. After several feedback loops, we were able to agree on a design screen together.

    For the "user testing" I developed different tasks that the test persons had to solve. The online tool "Maze" helped us to explain the scenario and the corresponding tasks, to share the test environment with all team members and to evaluate the testing afterwards. We asked all team members to do the testing over the Christmas holidays with a relative, to observe the test subjects accordingly and to share any interesting findings with us.

    When we received the results and feedback from all the test persons in the new year, we sat down together as a product team and defined various improvements. I then implemented these in the screens.

    At the end of the project, I was tasked with dividing the new design into small individual packages and creating a plan for the development from the old design to the new design. I then entered these individual packages as features into the company's internal tool "Product Board" so that the implementation of the new design can be planned into the roadmap. In addition, I familiarised one of the three developers with the entire new design and the path to the new design.

    The story behind the project

    I was able to familiarise myself with the entire project quite quickly. During the onboarding phase at Cliniserve, I received a detailed explanation of the patient app. This helped me a lot to understand the problems and think about possible solutions.

    In addition, I was given enough time by my supervisor to get to grips with Adobe Xd. It was fun to learn the tool and build the first screens myself.

    I found the communication in the team very beneficial. The weekly meetings always gave me new ideas and a good overview of my tasks. I really liked the fact that I could work independently and that I always received help with questions and problems.

    The biggest potential for improvement is in the overall planning of the project. Next time, I would like to have a shared platform for us as a product team to plan and document all the important steps, so that important insights and feedback don't get

    I really would have appreciated being involved in the actual design of the app, which was ultimately taken over by the permanent product designer due to the time pressure. This could have been prevented by clearer scheduling. In the course of the user testing, it became clear to me how important testing in person is. In the next round of testing, I would invest more time in organising live testing.
