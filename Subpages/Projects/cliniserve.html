    Redesign: Patient App

    With Cliniserve's Patient App, patients receiving hospital care can send requests to nursing staff. After several years of active use on the units within different hospitals, the overall look and structure of the app should be adapted and improved.

    Client

    Role

    Intern (Product Management)

    Services

    Wireframing

    Prototyping

    User testing

    The story behind the project

    During my practical semester in the product management department of Cliniserve, I had the opportunity to get to grips with the Patient App. It is used in various German hospitals.

    There are two different versions of the Patient App: on the one hand, there is the mobile smartphone app, and on the other, the so-called terminal app. This terminal app is used by patients who have a so-called bedside terminal. These terminals are tablets that are fixed to the corresponding hospital bed. Cliniserve has received an increasing amount of negative feedback, particularly with regard to the terminal app, which is why the product team decided to initiate a comprehensive redesign of the app.

    I was allowed to use my time as an intern and help the product team with this redesign. I would like to explain below how the entire project went, what the goals were and what challenges we encountered.

    Project goal

    Based on feedback from clients and caregivers, Cliniserve was able to identify several "pain points".


    Painpoints:

    1. Queries difficult to read
    2. Use of keyboard and scroll animations very error-prone
    3. Switching between already made requests and the menu not intuitive
    4. Uncertainty of patients whether a request has already been sent

    The aim of the project was to create a new design for the terminal app, taking these pain points into account, to test it and to prepare it for implementation.

    Process

    To get a feeling for the style of the app, I first clicked through the app and analysed where the user encounters the new feature. In addition, I collected first ideas for the style of the ring. The challenge regarding the ring was the requirement that the ring should look as real as possible.

    The story behind the project

    I was able to put myself in the user's shoes very quickly because I was used to using the app myself. At the beginning, I was particularly worried about designing the ring, as I was only familiar with a few programmes for modelling 3D objects, which are also rather tedious to learn. The tight time frame of the competition of one week did not allow for the necessary familiarisation. Due to these circumstances, I was and am very proud of the result of my ring, which I was able to achieve in only a few working hours in Vectary.

    In addition, the communication with the customer worked very well. The customer responded quickly and honestly and also contributed his own ideas.

    For a similar project I would try to structure my design screens better. I unfortunately failed to carefully name and group all the objects on my screen, which cost me a lot of time in later adjustments. With every improvement I had to make in Figma, I realised how important it is to build up your work well and to sort all objects neatly.

    In addition, for a next project I will take care to document my work steps better and to save important documents in a suitable place. This way, when you look back on your projects, you can better understand how your own process to the finished product went and analyse it better.
